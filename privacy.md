# Heaan Inc. Privacy Policy

**Last Updated:** 2026-04-30
**Version:** v1.0

To safeguard the privacy and rights of data subjects, **Heaan Inc.** ("Heaan"
or "the Company"), a Delaware corporation in the United States and a
wholly-owned subsidiary of **Cryptolab Inc.** (a corporation of the Republic
of Korea), responsibly processes and securely manages personal information in
accordance with applicable laws, including the Korean Personal Information
Protection Act ("PIPA"), the EU General Data Protection Regulation ("GDPR"),
and applicable U.S. state privacy laws (including the CCPA/CPRA).

This Privacy Policy describes how Heaan, in coordination with its parent
company Cryptolab Inc., processes personal information collected from users and
customers — including those acquired through Google Cloud Marketplace
listings — in order to provide enVector Cloud services and related sales,
billing, and support activities.

This notice supplements, and is consistent with, the Cryptolab Inc. enVector
Cloud Privacy Policy at
[https://envector.io/privacy-policy](https://envector.io/privacy-policy). It
governs Marketplace-specific data flows where Heaan acts as the seller of
record.

## 0. Roles of the Parties

| Party | Role |
|-------|------|
| Heaan Inc. (Delaware, USA) | **Marketplace seller of record** for Google Cloud Marketplace listings; processor of lead, billing, and entitlement data |
| Cryptolab Inc. (Republic of Korea) | Parent entity and **provider of the enVector Cloud service**; controller of service-level personal data; joint controller for Marketplace acquisition data |

Heaan Inc. and Cryptolab Inc. act as **joint controllers** for personal data
collected via Google Cloud Marketplace. Each party is responsible for
compliance with the laws applicable to its respective operations.

## 1. Purpose, Scope, and Duration of Personal Information Collection and Use

The Company collects and uses personal information only to the minimum extent
necessary, with the consent of the data subject where required and otherwise on
the legal bases listed below.

| Collected Items | Purpose | Legal Basis | Retention |
|-----------------|---------|-------------|-----------|
| Email address, Name | enVector Cloud account creation, authentication, service notifications | Consent (PIPA Art. 15(1)(1) / GDPR Art. 6(1)(a)); Contract necessity (GDPR Art. 6(1)(b)) | Until account deletion or expiration of legal retention obligations |
| **Marketplace lead-form data:** name, business email, company, job title, free-text message | Process inbound sales inquiries from Google Cloud Marketplace listings; respond to "Contact Sales" requests | Consent (GDPR Art. 6(1)(a)); Legitimate interest in responding to a prospective customer (GDPR Art. 6(1)(f)) | 24 months from the last interaction, or upon verified deletion request (whichever is sooner) |
| **Marketplace entitlement & billing data:** consumer project ID, entitlement ID, subscription metadata, usage metrics (e.g. `compute_hour`), billing address (provided by Google) | Provision the purchased plan, meter usage, reconcile billing with Google Cloud Marketplace | Contract necessity (GDPR Art. 6(1)(b)); Legal obligation (GDPR Art. 6(1)(c)) for tax / accounting records | 7 years (legal billing retention obligation) or as required by applicable tax law |

Upon a verified user request to delete an account, retained data will be erased
unless otherwise required to be kept under applicable laws.

## 2. Automatically Collected Information (Cookies, Logs, Tracking Technologies)

The Company may automatically collect the following information when users
access the service to ensure security, prevent fraud, and optimize service
performance:

- IP address, browser type and version
- Device and operating system information
- Service access records, user activity logs, and timestamps
- Authentication and session tokens
- Usage telemetry pushed by the application's metering agent (e.g.
  `envector/compute_hour`) to Google Cloud's Service Control endpoint for
  Marketplace billing purposes

Cookies or similar tracking technologies may be used for authentication,
analytics, and service optimization. Users in the EEA will be presented with a
cookie-consent mechanism where required by law. If you choose to disable or
refuse cookies, some parts of the Service may not function properly.

## 3. Procedure and Method of Destruction of Personal Information

The Company will promptly destroy personal information when it is no longer
necessary due to the expiration of the retention period or the achievement of
the processing purpose.

The Company identifies the personal information that requires destruction and
destroys it under the approval of the Privacy Officer. Destruction is performed
using commercially reasonable and technically possible measures so that the
information is irrecoverable or irreproducible:

- Electronic files containing personal information are permanently deleted
  using secure deletion or cryptographic erasure such that the files are
  irrecoverable.
- Physical records, printouts, and other recording media are shredded or
  incinerated.

If continued retention of personal information is required under applicable
laws despite expiration of the retention period or completion of the
processing purpose, such information is moved to a separate database or
retained in a different location.

## 4. Criteria for Determining Additional Use and Provision

In accordance with PIPA Articles 15(3) and 17(4), the Company may use or
provide personal information without the data subject's consent based on the
factors outlined in PIPA Enforcement Decree Article 14-2:

- Whether additional use or provision is relevant to the original purpose of
  collection
- Whether additional use or provision is foreseeable by data subjects
- Whether the interests of data subjects are unfairly infringed
- Whether necessary safety measures (such as pseudonymization or encryption)
  have been implemented

## 5. Security of Personal Information

To ensure the safety of personal information, the Company implements:

- **Managerial measures**: an internal management plan, dedicated organization,
  regular employee training, and privacy governance.
- **Technical measures**: access control to processing systems, access-control
  systems, encryption of personal information, and up-to-date security
  programs.
- **Physical measures**: controlled access to IT rooms, data-storage rooms, and
  other sensitive areas.

## 6. Rights of Data Subjects

At any time, a data subject may request that the Company access, correct,
delete, suspend the processing of, or withdraw consent for their personal
information, or refuse or seek explanation for an automated decision. The
Company is committed to taking immediate measures in response.

These rights may be limited by PIPA Articles 35(4) and 37(2). The Company
cannot delete personal information whose collection is required by other laws.

Pursuant to PIPA Article 38, you may exercise your rights via writing, email,
or facsimile, including through a representative or authorized agent (with
appropriate power of attorney).

## 7. Data Breach Notification

In the event of a personal data breach, the Company will notify affected users
and relevant authorities without undue delay, and where required under
applicable laws (including GDPR), within 72 hours of becoming aware of the
breach.

To facilitate service provision and improve user experience, the Company
entrusts the processing of personal information to external service providers.
The Company supervises these providers to ensure compliance with applicable
laws (including PIPA and GDPR) and the safety of personal information.

| Trustee | Items Transferred | Purpose | Retention |

### 8.3. Disclosure of Changes

If the contents of the entrusted work or the trustees change, the Company will
disclose the changes through this Privacy Policy or via the Company's website
without undue delay.

## 12. Additional Information for Users in the European Economic Area (EEA)

This section applies to users located in the EEA, the United Kingdom, and
Switzerland. It supplements the main Privacy Policy to comply with the GDPR.

### 12.1. Legal Basis for Processing

Pursuant to GDPR Article 6:

- **Contractual necessity**: processing is necessary for the performance of the
  contract to provide enVector Cloud services.
- **Legitimate interests**: processing is necessary for security, fraud
  prevention, sales contact (Marketplace leads), and service improvement.
- **Legal obligation**: compliance with legal obligations (e.g. tax, billing
  recordkeeping).
- **Consent**: where applicable; you may withdraw consent at any time.

### 12.2. International Data Transfers

The Company is headquartered in the Republic of Korea. On December 17, 2021
the European Commission adopted an adequacy decision for the Republic of
Korea; therefore, personal-data transfers from the EEA to Korea do not require
additional safeguards. Transfers to Heaan Inc. and other U.S. service
providers rely on Standard Contractual Clauses (SCCs) or equivalent transfer
mechanisms where required.

### 12.3. Your Rights under GDPR

In addition to Section 6, EEA residents have:

- **Right to rectification & erasure**: correct inaccurate data or request the
  "Right to be Forgotten."
- **Right to restriction & portability**: restrict processing or request data
  transfer to another controller.
- **Right to object**: object to processing based on legitimate interests.
- **Right to lodge a complaint**: with a supervisory authority in your member
  state.
